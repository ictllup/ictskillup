Information Processing Cycle

The series of input, process, output, and storage activities is very often called the information processing cycle.

Data

Data is a collection of raw and unprocessed facts, figures, and symbols.

  • Letters (e.g., a, b, c, ...)
  • Words and symbols
  • Numbers
  • Examination scores
  • Musical notes

Information

Information is data that is processed, organized, meaningful, and useful.

  • Words (e.g., apple, boy, cat)
  • A report, a letter, an essay
  • A mathematical formula
  • Comments, grades
  • A piece of music, a song

The Five Basic Stages of Information Processing

  1. Collection of Data
  2. Preparation of Data
  3. Input of Data
  4. Processing of Data
  5. Output of Information

Common Information Processing Systems

  • Batch Processing System
  • Real-time Processing System
  • Interactive Processing System
  • Multi-tasking System
  • Multi-user System
  • Time-sharing System

Advantages and Disadvantages of Using Computers for Information Processing

Advantages

  • Computers can share data and information with other computers.
  • Tasks can be completed faster.
  • Computers can process large amounts of data and generate error-free results.
  • Computers can store enormous amounts of data for future use.
  • High reliability of components.
  • Efficiency and productivity can be raised.
  • Running cost becomes lower in the long term.
  • Tasks can be completed with little human intervention.
  • Overall security can be raised.
  • Management can observe new information and new trends more quickly.
  • Customer services can be improved.

Disadvantages

  • Initial investment cost can be high.
  • Extra cost is required to employ specialized staff.
  • Some jobs may be lost due to computerization.
  • Some staff has to be trained or retrained.
  • Face-to-face interactions among staff may be reduced.
  • Easier transmission of viruses via the internet.
  • Abuse of personal information.
  • Failure of computers can bring an organization to a halt.
  • Security has to be provided to protect personnel and staff.

Considerations before Using Computers for Information Processing

  • Whether computerization can really solve the problem?
  • Is it cost effective in the long term?
  • Whether large amount of data has to be handled?
  • Is high processing speed really significant?
  • Can the present staff manage the system?
  • Will the change due to computerization lower the morale of staff?
  • How can the loss of jobs be handled properly?
  • What can be done to help staff adapt to computerization?
