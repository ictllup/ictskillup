Care and Safety for ICT Tools

General Care

  • Handle ICT tools with care to avoid damage
  • Keep devices clean and dust-free
  • Use protective cases or covers for devices
  • Store devices in a safe and secure location

Safety Precautions

  • Avoid overcharging batteries
  • Keep devices away from water and moisture
  • Avoid exposing devices to extreme temperatures
  • Use surge protectors to protect against power surges

  • Use certified and approved electrical cables and adapters
  • Avoid overloading power outlets
  • Keep electrical cables organized and secure
  • Avoid touching electrical components

Data Safety

  • Use strong and unique passwords
  • Enable encryption and secure boot
  • Regularly back up important data
  • Use antivirus software and firewalls

Physical Safety

  • Take regular breaks to avoid eye strain and fatigue
  • Use ergonomic furniture and accessories
  • Keep walkways clear of cables and devices
  • Avoid lifting heavy devices or equipment

  • Recycle devices and components when possible
  • Follow local regulations and guidelines for disposal

Computer Lab Rules

  • 1. Follow Lab Rules and Regulations: Familiarize yourself with the lab's rules and regulations and follow them strictly.
  • 2. Use Equipment and Devices Only for Authorized Purposes: Use lab equipment and devices only for authorized purposes and projects.
  • 3. Keep the Lab Clean and Organized: Keep the lab clean and organized, and dispose of trash and recyclables properly.
  • 4. Report Any Damage or Issues to Lab Staff: Report any damage or issues with equipment or devices to lab staff immediately.
  • 5. Use Strong and Unique Passwords: Use strong and unique passwords for all accounts, and keep them confidential.
  • 6. Be Aware of Malware and Viruses: Be aware of malware and viruses, and take steps to prevent them from infecting lab equipment and devices.
  • 7. Use Antivirus Software and Firewalls: Use antivirus software and firewalls to protect lab equipment and devices from malware and viruses.
  • 8. Avoid Suspicious Emails and Attachments: Avoid suspicious emails and attachments, and never open or download them.
  • 9. Respect Others' Work and Property: Respect others' work and property, and never tamper with or damage their projects or equipment.
  • 10. Log Off and Shut Down Equipment When Finished: Log off and shut down equipment when finished using it, to conserve energy and prevent damage.

ICT User Care and Safety

Physical Safety

  • Take regular breaks: Look away from your screen every 20 minutes to reduce eye strain. Get up from your desk to stretch and move around every hour.
  • Use ergonomic furniture: Adjust your chair, desk, and keyboard to comfortable positions. Use a headset or speakerphone for long phone calls.
  • Keep walkways clear: Avoid tripping hazards like cables, wires, and devices. Keep walkways clear and tidy.
  • Avoid lifting heavy devices: Lift devices correctly, bending at the knees and lifting with your legs. Ask for help if a device is too heavy.

Data Safety

  • Use strong and unique passwords: Use a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using easily guessable information like names or birthdays.
  • Enable encryption and secure boot: Protect your device and data with encryption and secure boot. Use a Trusted Platform Module (TPM) if available.
  • Regularly back up important data: Use an external hard drive, cloud storage, or a backup service. Test your backups to ensure they are complete and recoverable.
  • Use antivirus software and firewalls: Install and regularly update antivirus software and firewalls. Use a reputable security suite or software.

Online Safety

  • Be cautious with links and attachments: Avoid suspicious emails, links, and attachments. Verify the sender and purpose before clicking or downloading.
  • Avoid suspicious emails and messages: Be wary of phishing scams, spam, and malware. Report suspicious emails and messages to the relevant authorities.
  • Use secure protocols for online transactions: Use HTTPS (SSL/TLS) for online transactions. Verify the website's security certificate and reputation.
  • Keep personal information private: Avoid sharing personal information online, especially sensitive information like financial data or passwords.

Device Safety

  • Use protective cases or covers: Protect your device from scratches, drops, and spills. Use a screen protector and case or cover.
  • Avoid exposing devices to extreme temperatures: Keep devices away from direct sunlight, heat sources, and extreme cold.
  • Keep devices clean and dust-free: Regularly clean your device with a soft cloth and avoid eating or drinking near it.
  • Use surge protectors: Protect your device from power surges and spikes. Use a surge protector or uninterruptible power supply (UPS).

Cyberbullying and Harassment

  • Report incidents: Report any incidents of cyberbullying or harassment to the relevant authorities, such as your school or workplace.
  • Block or report suspicious users: Block or report users who are suspicious or abusive. Use privacy settings to control who can contact you.
  • Keep personal information private: Avoid sharing personal information online, especially sensitive information like financial data or passwords.
  • Use secure and respectful communication channels: Use secure and respectful communication channels, such as encrypted messaging apps or email.

Digital Citizenship

  • Respect others' online presence: Treat others with respect and kindness online. Avoid cyberbullying, harassment, or online abuse.
  • Use respectful and inclusive language: Use respectful and inclusive language online, avoiding hate speech, profanity, or discriminatory language.
  • Follow online community
  • Follow online community guidelines: Follow online community guidelines, rules, and terms of service. Respect the community and its members.
  • Report online incidents or concerns: Report any online incidents or concerns, such as cyberbullying, harassment, or online abuse, to the relevant authorities.
